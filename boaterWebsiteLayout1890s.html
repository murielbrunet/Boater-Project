From the River to the Runway

~ A Digital Exploration of the Boater Hat ~

1890s

During the 1890s, there was a promotion of of Victorian masculinity in women’s activities and appearance. This was largely due to the Muscular Christianity movement which was founded in this period on the idea that a proper Christian must be both spiritually and physically fit.
Women were now allowed and even encouraged to do masculine things such as play a game of tennis, go sailing, and participate in a new sport called basketball. For these physical activities women needed new attire, and so they adapted it from men’s wear, sometimes by taking a piece of men’s clothing and simply putting it on.
One such piece was the boater hat--a hat that had become popular as semiformal sportswear for men. Initially, the women’s boater hat was identical to the men’s, but this changed rapidly. First, the hats became smaller to fit women’s hair better. Soon there were boater hats for all occasions and classes--fancy ones for a upperclass lady to wear around town, simpler ones with a nice grosgrain ribbon for a stroll on the beach, or mannish, plain ones for the working woman.
Displayed above is a 3D model of a boater hat circa 1890 from the Smith College Historic Dress Archives that is also displayed in the vitrine behind you.

Tennis Outfit

When lawn tennis first became a thing it was quite leisurely and slow compared to today's tennis matches. This meant that not only was it a sport allowed to be played amongst women, but that one could also keep a hat on while playing. What better hat could there be than a simple straw boater?
-
Peterson’s Magazine, 1888
Taken from "When Girls Came Out to Play"
